Adoption Across Company Size

Developer Sentiment: The Trust Deficit

Despite massive adoption, developers are growing more skeptical of AI's reliability. The initial hype is giving way to a more nuanced understanding of its limitations, leading to a significant gap between tool usage and trust in its output.

Trust in AI is Falling

While more developers are using AI tools, the percentage who trust the accuracy of the output has decreased year-over-year.

The "Almost Right" Problem

66%

of developers struggle with AI solutions that are close, but ultimately incorrect.

This leads to one of the most cited pain points: debugging AI-generated code often takes longer than writing it from scratch.

Enterprise Integration Hurdles

Deploying AI coding tools at scale is not a simple plug-and-play solution. Organizations must navigate critical issues related to security, code quality, and the inherent limitations of AI in understanding complex, proprietary systems.

The Evolving Engineer

AI is not replacing developers, but it is fundamentally reshaping their roles and the skills required for success. The value of senior engineers is increasingly shifting toward architecture, mentorship, and critical oversight.

Productivity Gains by Experience

Junior developers see the largest productivity gains on routine tasks, while senior developers see more modest gains, reflecting their focus on complex, less-automatable problems.
